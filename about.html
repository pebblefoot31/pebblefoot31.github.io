so, why computer science?

My interdisciplinary interests and passion for solving puzzles drove me towards this field. As an individual that welcomes the challenges at the system-level of a computer, I am interested in embedded systems (what makes my calculator work? what about the timer on my microwave? and autonomous driving? the list goes on!). As an artist and visual thinker, I am equally fascinated by web development. Whether it be desiging a website with a focus on elevating the user experience or contributing to intelligent, energy-saving light automation systems, my ultimate objective is to wield software engineering as a tool for improving the human condition.

my core: all forms of creativity

Painting. Drawing. Jewelry-making. Beautiful note-taking. Ever since I could pick up a pencil (or paintbrush), I have had the insuppressible itch to create fine art. In recent years, my favorite medium has been color pencils of the Prismacolor brand, due to their smooth, buttery texture and vibrant pigment overlays. To me, art is destressing, and keeps me in touch with the colorful, free-range part of my mind. Read more about how art helps in everyday life in my "art is life" blog post! My favorite pieces will be up on this site soon-- stay tuned.

gym and books and stuff

I love a routine. More importantly-- I need the gym in any routine. Read more about how I figured out a gym routine and my strategies for protein intake as a vegetarian in my "how to gym" blog post. I also read, when I get the chance to. Drama (especially tragedies) are my favorite because where's the fun in constantly absorbing perfect storybook endings? Personally, I enjoy plots that reflect a beloved characters sorrow right off the pages, and into a readers' comprehension. Hence, my favorite author is Khaled Hosseini, author of masterpieces 'The Kite Runner' and 'A Thousand Splendid Suns'. Some more favorites include...

    - The Tattooist of Auschwitz by Heather Morris.
    - Fahrenheit 451 by Ray Bradbury.
    - The Joy Luck Club by Amy Tan.
