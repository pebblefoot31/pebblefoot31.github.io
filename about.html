ABOUT

Hi, I’m Nia! I am an undergraduate student studying computer science and data science at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville (UTK). So far, I have had experiences with web development and scientific computing (head over to my blog to learn more about my past work). In my free time, I run a small henna art business where I can slow down, work with my hands, and connect with people in an entirely different way. Although I am currently living in the states with my family, I have previously lived on three different continents and visited several countries– a list I look forward to expanding. I like to unwind by spending time with loved ones, baking, exercising, and creating art. My favorite mediums to work with include oil paint, acrylic paint, and color pencils. As an artist, I love finding the beauty in things around me, whether it’s elegant lines of code or a peaceful mountain view. This is a space where I share the aspects of life that inspire and captivate me.

why computer science?

My interdisciplinary interests and love for solving puzzles naturally led me to computer science. I’m drawn to the challenges found at the system level and fascinated by how things work under the hood, from microwave timers and calculators to embedded systems that power autonomous vehicles. As a visual thinker, I also enjoy exploring web development and design. There’s something deeply satisfying about bringing an idea to life through tools like Figma and translating it into smooth digital experiences.
